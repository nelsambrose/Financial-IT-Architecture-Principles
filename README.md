## Why This Exists

Financial institutions operate under unique pressures — regulatory scrutiny, zero tolerance for downtime, complex data governance requirements, and the constant need to balance innovation with prudence. This guide distils the core IT architecture principles that address those pressures directly.

Free to use as a reference, share with your team, or use as a study resource.

---

## Principles Covered

| # | Principle | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Secure by Design | Security built in from the ground up |
| 2 | Interoperability | Seamless communication across systems |
| 3 | Scalability & Flexibility | Handle growth without disruption |
| 4 | Data Integrity & Confidentiality | Protect and trust your data |
| 5 | Regulatory Compliance | Meet financial regulatory requirements |
| 6 | Business Continuity | Minimise downtime, plan for failure |
| 7 | Business Alignment | Architecture that serves business goals |
| 8 | Cloud-Native Architecture | Modern, scalable cloud design |
| 9 | Strategic Technology Balance | Innovation without reckless risk |
| 10 | User-Centric Design | Systems built around the people using them |
| 11 | Efficiency | Optimal use of resources |
| 12 | Data Governance | Clear ownership and data quality standards |
| 13 | Prudent Innovation Strategy | Adopt new tech thoughtfully |
| 14 | Cost-Effectiveness | Deliver value without waste |
| 15 | Future-Proofing | Build for tomorrow, not just today |
| 16 | Modularity & Flexibility | Loosely coupled, easy to evolve |
| 17 | Automation | Reduce manual effort and human error |
| 18 | Testability | Systems you can verify and trust |
| 19 | Consistency | Predictable, reliable behaviour across systems |
